Stevie Ray Vaughan

Legendary Guitarist and Frontman of Blues Rock Trio: Double Trouble.

Biography

Stephen Ray Vaughan (October 3, 1954 – August 27, 1990) was an American musician, singer, songwriter, and record producer, best known as the guitarist and frontman of the blues rock trio Stevie Ray Vaughan and Double Trouble. Although his mainstream career only spanned seven years, he is considered an icon and one of the most influential musicians in the history of blues music, and one of the greatest guitarists of all time.

Timeline

  • 1972 - SRV dropped out of high school and moved to Austin. At this time he had started playing local gigs to acquire some recognition.
  • 1978 - Stevie started a band with Tommy Shannon(Bassist) and Chris Layton(Drummer) called "Double Trouble." The band became popular and quickly found a name for themselves in Texas.
  • 1982 - Stevie Ray Vaughan and Double Trouble preform at Montreux Music Festival. David Bowie had been at this festival and contacted SRV to collaborate on his song "Lets Dance."
  • 1983 - Stevie signs a record deal with Epic Records.
  • 1983 - Stevie Ray Vaughan and Double Trouble release their first studio album with Epic Records; "Texas Flood."
  • 1989 - His fourth studio album "In Step" reaches rank number 33 in the United States. He recieved his only number one record with this album; "Crossfire."
  • 1989 - He became one of the most demanded blues preformers in the world and was able to headline at Madison Square Garden.
  • 1990 - He attended Beale Street Music Festival.
  • 1990 * - On August 27th Stevie Ray Vaughan along with 4 other people had died in a helicopter crash in East Troy, Wisconsin.
  • 2003 - Stevie Ray Vaughan was ranked the number 7th guitarist of all time by David Frickle of Rolling Stone Magazine.
  • 2015 - Stevie Ray Vaughan and Double Trouble were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
